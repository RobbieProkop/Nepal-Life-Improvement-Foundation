Why We Started

Nepal Life Improvement Foundation officially formed in 2019. Prior to this, our founder Maury Mason, had been helping those in the community of Pachabaya, a village 200 kilometers west of Kathmandu, near the city of Pokhara since 2015. The contributions were to help people recover from the earthquake damage and resulting economic hardships of that year. The first projects began with rebuilding and repairing damaged homes and expanded to projects improving village infrastructure including pathways, stairs and connecting homes to water and hydro. In November 2019, along with Robbie Prokop we were officially incorporated as a Canadian Non Profit Organization. In 2020 Lilly Mason came on board, and helped to expand our fundraising base which has enabled us to take on more projects.

Current Focus

Establishment of NLIF Headquaters

With many projects already complete, and many more projects to come, a headquarters was needed. We were fortunate to secure a 2 year lease of a building that was not being used. The money from our rent will go towards supporting a family in the village. Monthly donations will help us to pay for the headquarters, and with an extra 2 rooms, we are now able to house volunteers.
