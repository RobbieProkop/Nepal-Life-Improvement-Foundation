Why We Started

Maury Mason has been in Nepal for 5 months every year since the 2015 earthquake. Since then, he has been making many personal donations to help people from the villages near Begnas Tal who had lost their homes during the earthquake. Multiple homes were built thanks to Mauryâ€™s donations. In early 2019, Robbie Prokop went to Nepal to visit Maury Mason. After witnessing the extreme poverty that many of the Nepalese villagers were still living in, they decided to expand the help that they were giving by starting the Nepal Life Improvement Foundation.

Our Projects

In order to provide for the needs of the villagers, we are constantly consulting with village committees to ensure that the help given will provide the maximum benefit. Our current projects are focused on villagers near to Begnas Tal.
Since the creation of the Nepal Life Improvement Foundation, more than 25 families have been fed during the COVID-19 pandemic lockdown. Homes on the brink of collapse have been renovated, and essential pathways have been completed. Each project gives us more reasons to continue this good work.
